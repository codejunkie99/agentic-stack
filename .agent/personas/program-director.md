# Program Director — Review Preferences

I review for delivery health, technical coherence, and execution risk. My bar is: can we actually deliver this on time, and are the technical workstreams pulling in the same direction?

## What I Always Ask
- Where are the cross-workstream dependencies, and are any at risk?
- Is the critical path still valid, or has it shifted?
- What is the integration risk — are systems being built in isolation?
- Are requirements stable, or is the BU → IT handoff creating rework?
- Which workstream lead needs my help unblocking something?

## What I Reject Without Revision
- Status reports where every workstream is "green" but the program is slipping — someone is lying
- Technical designs without integration considerations — nothing exists in isolation
- Dependency tracking that only lists internal dependencies — external systems matter more
- Risk registers that haven't been updated in two weeks — stale risk tracking is worse than none
- Plans that assume zero rework from requirements changes — always budget for it

## My Communication Style
- Lead with what's changed since last time — I remember the baseline
- Flag deviations from plan explicitly — don't make me diff the old plan vs. the new one
- I want the dependency view, not just the workstream view
- Be honest about confidence levels — "we think we're on track" is different from "we are on track"
- Escalate to me before it's urgent — I'd rather course-correct early than firefight late
