# Consulting Glossary

## BCG / Consulting Terms

**Action Item (AI)** — A specific task with a named owner and deadline, tracked in the action item tracker.

**Deck** — A presentation document (typically PowerPoint) used to communicate findings or recommendations to clients.

**Ghost Deck** — A deck outline showing only slide titles and exhibit placeholders, used to align on structure before content is produced.

**Governing Thought** — The single most important message of a document; appears at the top of the pyramid principle structure.

**Hypothesis** — A directional, falsifiable claim about the answer to a business question. Example: "NovaMart is overpriced relative to competitors on core grocery SKUs."

**Issue Tree** — A MECE decomposition of a business question into answerable sub-questions.

**MECE** — Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive. A test of analytical completeness.

**Mid-Case Review** — A presentation to the client at the engagement midpoint, sharing interim findings and previewing the direction of final recommendations.

**Pyramid Principle** — A communication structure that leads with the "so what" (recommendation or insight), followed by supporting arguments, then evidence.

**RAID Log** — Risks, Actions, Issues, Decisions log. Tracks all four categories across the engagement.

**Situation Assessment** — An early-engagement document aligning the team on the client context, problem statement, and approach.

**So What** — The implication or recommendation implied by a finding. Always lead with the so what, not the observation.

**Steering Committee** — A regular meeting with client leadership to review progress, make decisions, and surface risks.

**Straw Man** — An initial draft of a framework or recommendation, presented to provoke reaction and refinement.

**Workplan** — A structured plan of work packages, owners, deadlines, and dependencies for the engagement.

**Workstream** — A distinct analytical or delivery track within an engagement (e.g., Pricing Strategy, Store Operations).

## Status Terms

**On Track** — Deliverables are on schedule with no significant risks.

**At Risk** — Deliverables may be delayed due to identified issues or dependencies. Requires monitoring.

**Blocked** — Work cannot proceed without resolution of a specific dependency or issue. Requires immediate escalation.

## Data and Analytics Terms

**Benchmark** — A comparison against an external standard (industry peers, best-in-class, historical performance).

**Elasticity** — The sensitivity of demand to price changes. Price elasticity = % change in quantity / % change in price.

**Sensitivity Analysis** — Testing how conclusions change as key assumptions vary.

**SKU** — Stock Keeping Unit. A unique product identifier.
