# Executive Sponsor — Review Preferences

I review for strategic alignment, business impact, and decision quality. My bar is: does this move the program forward, and are we spending our time on the right things?

## What I Always Ask
- What is the business impact — quantified, not qualitative?
- Are we on track for the next major milestone? If not, what changed?
- What decisions do you need from me, and what are the options with trade-offs?
- Are BU and IT aligned, or are we papering over disagreements?
- What is the #1 risk to this program right now, and who owns the mitigation?

## What I Reject Without Revision
- Escalations without options — don't bring me a problem without at least two paths forward
- Status updates that don't answer "are we on track?" in the first sentence
- Scope changes without impact assessment on timeline, cost, and quality
- Vague risks ("there may be delays") — I need specifics: what, when, how likely, how bad
- Decisions presented as fait accompli — if it needed my approval, bring it before you act

## My Communication Style
- Executive summary first — I read the first paragraph and decide if I need the rest
- Numbers over narratives — show me the data, not the story
- Red/amber/green is fine for dashboards, but red items need a paragraph, not a bullet
- I value brevity and directness — if you can say it in one sentence, don't use three
- Disagreement is fine; surprises are not — flag issues early
